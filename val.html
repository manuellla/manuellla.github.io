Autonomous Navigation & SLAM Integration

From Clearpath Jackal Simulations to Full-Scale Vehicle Deployment

Overview

This project focuses on the development and deployment of a robust navigation stack for autonomous mobile robots and full-scale vehicles. By leveraging ROS 1 Noetic, I worked on a pipeline that transitions from high-fidelity simulation to real-world execution on both a Clearpath Jackal UGV and a modified Chevrolet Spin.

Technical Stack & Implementation

  • Core Framework: ROS 1 Noetic (C++ & Python)
  • Simulation: Gazebo (for Jackal kinematics) and CARLA (for urban environment testing)
  • Localization & Mapping: Implementation of SLAM algorithms for environment mapping and real-time localization.
  • Navigation: GNSS Waypoint Following and obstacle avoidance using fused sensor data.
  • Data Analysis: Conducted extensive data rounds to optimize path-planning efficiency and sensor accuracy.

Real-World Deployment: Chevrolet Spin

The culmination of this research involved implementing the validated navigation solution into a Chevrolet Spin. In this setup, the vehicle successfully navigated autonomously through GNSS-defined paths. For safety and compliance, the system controlled steering and throttle, while a human safety driver remained in charge of the braking system.

Role: Robotics Developer Environment: Linux (Ubuntu 20.04)
